Dualism

Yoga carries with it the philosophy that the spirit, the mind and the body are one. The name is rooted in the view of the mind and body being dual, and interconnected.

The purpose of 'Dualism' is to provide access to yoga, as a way of life, that truly bridges the gap and brings harmony between mind and body.

What is yoga?

Yoga focuses on bringing harmony between the mind and body, it is a group of physical, mental and spiritual practices aimed to discipline the body and mind.

Benefits

Improves flexibility

Even the lowest intensity styles increase flexibility

Helps relieve stress

A combination of frequent movement-based yoga therapy and breathing-based exercises can significantly relieve anxiety.

Increases strength

In one small study, yoga practitioners were shown to have increased bone density in their spine and hips, compared to people in a control group.

Relieves depression

In a study of coal miners with chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, or C.O.P.D., yoga was shown to alleviate depression and anxiety.

Improves brain functioning

A database from the National Institutes of Health found that practicing yoga activated areas of the brain responsible for motivation, executive functioning and neuroplasticity.
